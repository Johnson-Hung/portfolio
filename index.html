New Project (February, 2020)

"New Project" is a multiplayer game(PC). In this game, you play as a living image in sliders, and realize that you are trapped. Not willing to stay in the application, you have to be ahead of others, take advantage of everything in the sliders to climb up, and be the first one to break out.

After taking some courses in computer networks and discrete mathematics, I applied what I learned to game development, and the game was born. In this project, I used Photon PUN2 plugin to implement the whole network system; it supported my game with stable connections to the nearest servers, and whenever players want to play with friends, they do not need to know their IP addresses or set up hamachi LAN, all the connecting processes can be done just by clicking the mousebutton.
Moreover, I created some additional functions like services for private rooms and kicking players as a room master. For a better in-game experience, I also put lots of effort into movement prediction and synchronization to make sure that the game runs smoothly for all clients with decent ping, preventing the possibilities of jittering or teleporting.
In a nutshell, the "New Project" helps me review some past lessons and integrate knowledge from multiple subjects. I am extremely excited to learn more about programming and networking in the future!

SAVE - 羊 (August, 2019)

"SAVE" is a free and creative puzzle game. In this game, you are the white lamb trying to save all the black lambs and escape from the wolf. 

This is my first mobile game, and I spent around one month to finish this project. During the process, there was one thing that I remembered and enjoyed the most, that is, learning A* and implementing it to my AI (wolf). I had heard of the term "A*" in a game design course before, but I didn't understand it at all. Thanks to this project, I finally learned what A* is and how to use it.

BALLSHOOOT (Oct-Dec, 2018)

BALLSHOOOT is a multiplayer game (PC). Players use three different types of magic balls to compete with each other, and the last one standing wins.

This is my first game; before BALLSHOOOT, I had no experience in game development. Actually, the game is my project in a game design course held by NTU IM (students were asked to make a game in one semester). I teamed up with my friend, Tim, who was responsible for art design, and together we finished this game in three months. I learned a lot from this project, including how to code in C#, how to use Unity and how UNET works. I obtained all the information from the Internet, and step by step, I finally got the work done. Special thanks to Tim for helping me with art, I really appreciate it!
